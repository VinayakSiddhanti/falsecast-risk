Management of False Smut of Rice

1. Use of Resistant/Tolerant Varieties
Prefer varieties known for tolerance or partial resistance to false smut as identified by regional trials.
Avoid highly susceptible high-yielding hybrids in endemic areas.

2. Use of Disease-Free and Treated Seed
Select healthy, smut-free seed from disease-free fields.
Treat seed with fungicides such as Carbendazim 2 g/kg or Tebuconazole 1 g/kg before sowing to eliminate seed-borne inoculum.

3. Crop Rotation and Field Sanitation
Rotate rice with non-host crops for at least one season.
Remove and destroy infected panicles and stubble after harvest to reduce primary inoculum.

4. Balanced Fertilization and Nutrient Management
Avoid excessive nitrogen application, especially late in the crop season, as it increases disease susceptibility.
Apply balanced doses of N:P:K and micronutrients (Zn, Si) to enhance host resistance.

5. Optimum Sowing Time and Crop Density
Adjust sowing dates to avoid conditions favorable for infection (high humidity, frequent rains during flowering).
Maintain recommended plant spacing to ensure good aeration and reduce canopy humidity.

6. Fungicidal Sprays at Critical Stages
Apply fungicides at booting to early panicle emergence stage (75–85 days after transplanting):

  • Propiconazole 25 EC @ 0.1%
  • Tebuconazole 25 EC @ 1 ml/L
  • Hexaconazole 5 EC @ 2 ml/L
  • Trifloxystrobin + Tebuconazole 75 WG @ 0.5 g/L

A second spray after 10–12 days can be given in high-risk fields.
